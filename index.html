The Gratuitous Gram is a food tent art installation that questions the nutrition facts label and how it frames the way we think about our meals. The label defines a meal as a set of gross, gram-based measurements of a few components. The way we choose to label our foods both reflects and shapes the way we think about food.

We, along with anyone that stops by the tent, will be reconstructing iconic dishes using the nutrition facts as a recipe. The result, while technically edible, would never be mistaken for the real dish. Why then does the nutrition facts label act as the basis for our understanding of health and nutrition?

Come out and make your own dish!

The installation is a collaboration between Elaine Reynolds – Professor of Biology at Lafayette – and Michael Hadley – Chicago-based artist and Lafayette alumnus – with support from Lafayette's Center for Community Engagement.
